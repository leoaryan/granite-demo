## Task 0 — Executive Summary: The View

A clean beat-and-improve print. Net sales rose 10% y/y to $308.3M and adjusted EBITDA jumped 39% to $24.6M (margin 8.0% vs 6.3%, +167bps) on higher shipments, better price/mix and fixed-cost leverage [book]. GAAP EPS $0.13 (vs $0.03) and adjusted EPS $0.18 (vs $0.07) [book] — the $0.18 lands exactly on the $0.18 Street estimate, and revenue $308.3M is in line with the ~$308M consensus [SE-SS]. The story is demand-led: the order book is up ~40% y/y (~90k more tons), A&D shipments more than doubled (tons +106%), Section 232 tariffs (50% on imported primary steel) are a structural tailwind, and a new bloom reheat furnace (150 t/hr vs 100 legacy) ramps in 3Q [book]. The watch-items: Q1 profit was helped by a $4.9M other-income line, energy costs stepped up y/y (favorable electricity contract expired May 2025), and the Q2 guide is only 'modestly higher' with price/mix flat until 2H. The Street is constructive — Overweight, $22.50 PT — yet the stock fell ~4% on the day [SE-SS].

## Task 5 — Earnings Call: Management Discussion & Q&A

### Section 1 — Management Discussion (prepared remarks)

- **Quarter framing:** Net sales +10% y/y to $308.3M; adj EBITDA +39% to $24.6M; shipments +11% sequentially — driven by demand improvement across end-markets [book]
  > "Adjusted EBITDA for the quarter totaled $24.6 million, reflecting a 39% increase compared to the prior year's first quarter." — CEO Mike Williams
- **Demand & tariffs:** Order book up ~40% y/y (~90k more tons); lead times into late 3Q; Section 232 50% tariff on imported primary steel a structural tailwind; April-26 tariff update did not affect Metallus's primary-steel products [book]
- **Capital projects:** New bloom reheat furnace demonstrated ~150 t/hr vs ~100 t/hr legacy; fully operational early-mid 3Q, roller furnace late 3Q — expected to improve operating leverage [book]
- **A&D growth:** A&D a key source of strength; tons more than doubled y/y; new rocket-motor tubing contract; ~$250M annualized run-rate expectation maintained [book]
- **Q2 outlook:** Q2 adj EBITDA 'modestly higher' q/q and y/y; shipments up low-single-digit %; price/mix similar to Q1 with 2H improvement; ~$2M sequential cost improvement (net of USW labor) [book]

### Section 2 — Q&A (analyst inquiries)

**1. Inventory build & order book**  *(John Franzreb, Sidoti & Co.)*
- **Concern:** Why the sizable rise in inventory — is it demand-driven or a build for a specific reason?
- **Response:**
  - Inventory is built in Q1 against the Q2 order-book demand, with lead times into mid-to-late 3Q [book]
  - Order book is over 40% greater y/y — about 90,000 more tons than the same time last year [book]
- **Takeaway:** The build is demand-positioning, not a glut — the ~40%/90k-ton order-book figure is the single most important durability data point in the quarter.

**2. Sequential cost improvement**  *(John Franzreb, Sidoti & Co.)*
- **Concern:** Is the ~$2M Q2 operational improvement above the new union-contract cost, or net of it?
- **Response:**
  - It is net of the increased USW labor costs — a net positive $2M off the higher wage base [book]
- **Takeaway:** Management confirmed the ~$2M is a clean net tailwind, so Q2 cost leverage is real even after the labor step-up.

**3. A&D $250M run-rate vs facility slip**  *(Samuel McKinney, KeyBanc Capital Markets)*
- **Concern:** The Army's munitions partner won't begin production until 2027 — does that jeopardize the $250M annualized A&D run-rate this year?
- **Response:**
  - Still expect to hit the $250M run-rate; the partner is at ~70k shells/month now (up from ~50k six months ago) vs a 100k target [book]
  - Offsetting the slip with other weapon-system programs, a new contract, and non-US/offshore demand; CFO framed $250M as a run-rate to achieve in the year, acknowledging lumpiness [book]
- **Takeaway:** The run-rate is reaffirmed but now leans on offsetting programs and is admittedly lumpy — the swing factor for the A&D growth thesis and a key thing to verify in coming quarters.

**4. Lead times & order-book profile**  *(Dave Storms, Stonegate Capital Markets)*
- **Concern:** Are late-3Q lead times a high watermark, and is order-book growth driven by price, mix, or volume?
- **Response:**
  - Demand expected to stay strong; orders coming in at a good weekly rate, visibility halfway through 3Q [book]
  - Order-book growth is overall volume-driven; biggest 2H opportunity is steady growth in industrial end-markets [book]
- **Takeaway:** Management says the growth is volume-led (not just price) — consistent with the flat base-sales/ton — which supports the durability case but means margin upside depends on the 2H price actions landing.

**5. Energy electricity contract**  *(Aaron Reed, Northcoast Research)*
- **Concern:** Terms of the new electricity contract after the favorable one expired — spot or long-term?
- **Response:**
  - 70% of electrical demand is fixed under a two-year agreement (now in the second six months of year one); the other 30% is spot-purchased [book]
- **Takeaway:** The y/y utility-cost headwind is now largely locked (70% fixed for two years), reducing energy-cost volatility risk going forward.

**Read:** A confident 'demand is real and durable' call with a credible buyer in every analyst — the questions clustered on the order book (Sidoti), the A&D run-rate vs the slipped Army facility (KeyBanc), order-book composition (Stonegate) and the energy contract (Northcoast) [book]. Management's answers were specific and numbers-backed (40%/90k-ton order book, ~$2M net cost improvement, 70k-vs-100k shells, 70% fixed power), which is a strength; the soft spot they kept circling back to is that pricing realization and the furnace ramp are 2H events.
