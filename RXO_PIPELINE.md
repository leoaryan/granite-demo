## Task 0 — Executive Summary: The View

A trough-cycle print in a soft freight market — revenue roughly flat at $1,425M (−0.6%) but gross margin compressed to 14.2% from 16.0% (−181bps, with Brokerage at 11.4%) and adjusted EBITDA fell to $6M from $22M [book]. The quarter itself is weak, but the forward signal is better: Q2 adjusted-EBITDA guide of $27M+ is above the ~$24M Street bar [book], spot mix rose to 33% (+600bps) and contract rates are guided to high-single-digit increases [book]. The bull case is a freight-cycle inflection plus Coyote synergy; the bear case is that brokerage volume still fell 8% and the cycle hasn't actually turned.

### Section 2 — Q&A (analyst inquiries)

**1. Normalized earnings power**  *(Brandon Oglenski, Barclays)*
- **Concern:** Even the Q2 high-end is down y/y despite much higher spot mix — what's the right normalized earnings power for this business?
- **Response:**
  - Management pointed to spot-mix conversion + contract-rate increases as the path back to mid-cycle EBITDA [book]
  - Q2 $27M+ is the first step up from the $6M trough; not yet normalized [book]
- **Takeaway:** The bull/bear hinges entirely on this — management is asking the Street to underwrite a mid-cycle recovery that the trough print doesn't yet show.

**2. Spot-mix margin conversion**  *(analyst-raised)*
- **Concern:** Higher spot mix historically drives incremental gross profit — why didn't margin expand in Q1?
- **Response:**
  - Carrier-rate inflation ran ahead of contract repricing this quarter, compressing the spread [book]
  - As contracts reset higher (high-single-digit), the spot tailwind converts to margin in 2H [book]
- **Takeaway:** There's a timing lag — the margin upside is real but deferred; watch 2H gross margin vs the 11.4% Q1 print.

**Read:** The call is a 'the bottom is in' message with a forward burden of proof — analysts pressed on normalized earnings and margin conversion, and management's answer is the Q2 guide plus a 2H rate reset, neither of which is delivered yet. [book]
