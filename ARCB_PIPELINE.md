*Integrated logistics company operating two segments: Asset-Based (ABF Freight — national/regional LTL trucking with ~240 service centers and an owned fleet, the larger segment at ~$655M Q1 revenue) and Asset-Light (truckload brokerage, managed transportation, and expedite — a '3PL with assets' model, ~$378M Q1 revenue). ## Task 0 — Executive Summary: The View

A messy-but-improving trough print in a still-soft freight market. Consolidated revenue grew ~3% to ~$1.0B, but profitability fell: non-GAAP operating income $13.5M vs $17.3M (−22%) and non-GAAP EPS $0.32 vs $0.51 (−37%), as the Asset-Based operating ratio deteriorated 140bps to 97.3% — higher cost per shipment (union wages, fuel, depreciation) outran a modest revenue-per-shipment gain [book]. The forward signal is the bull case: management guided Q2 Asset-Based OR to improve 400–500bps sequentially (vs ~350bps normal seasonality), with April tonnage +5% and rev/shipment +10% [book]. Asset-Light is the quiet win — $2.8M non-GAAP OI, already nearly double all of 2025's $1.5M [book]. The bear case: weight-per-shipment is mix/fuel-driven, not yet demand-driven, and EPS still fell sharply y/y.

## Task 5 — Earnings Call: Management Discussion & Q&A

### Section 1 — Management Discussion (prepared remarks)

- **Cycle position:** CEO framed a 'challenging' quarter (severe winter weather, higher fuel, uncertainty) but conditions 'becoming more constructive'; manufacturing PMI in expansion and truckload capacity exiting [book]
  > "demand trends that have started to stabilize, though overall level still remain below mid-cycle norms" — CEO Seth Runser
- **Consolidated results:** Revenue ~$1.0B (+3% y/y); non-GAAP OI $13M vs $17M; non-GAAP EPS $0.32 vs $0.51 — CFO Beasley [book]
- **Asset-Based:** Rev $655M (+2%/day); OR 97.3% (+140bps y/y, +110bps seq); tonnage/day +7%; deferred renewals +6.3%, best since 3Q22 [book]
- **Asset-Light:** Rev $378M (+7%/day); non-GAAP OI $2.8M vs $1.5M for all of FY25; SG&A/shipment −15% (record low), shipments/person/day +26% (record) [book]
- **Q2 + 2028 outlook:** Q2 Asset-Based OR to improve ~400–500bps seq (vs ~350bps normal); Asset-Light Q2 OI ~$1M–$3M; reaffirmed 2028 targets (OR 87–90%, EPS $12–$15) [book]

### Section 2 — Q&A (analyst inquiries)

**1. Q2 OR outperforming seasonality**  *(Scott H. Group, Wolfe Research)*
- **Concern:** With the Q2 OR guide outperforming seasonality, how much is fuel flow-through vs tonnage getting better, and is there anything different about fuel this time?
- **Response:**
  - CFO Beasley: outperformance is 'across the board' — rev/day, shipments/day, tonnage, weight/shipment, rev/cwt all above the 10-year history [book]
  - Fuel is a driver but NOT the primary one — 'we still would have been within our guidance range… even without the fuel changes'; ~350bps is normal seasonality, the strength takes it to 400–500bps [book]
- **Takeaway:** Management is explicitly de-emphasizing fuel as the swing factor — the OR upside is attributed to broad commercial/yield strength, which is what the bull case needs to be real rather than a fuel mirage.

**2. Truckload pricing into mid-cycle**  *(Jason H. Seidl, TD Cowen)*
- **Concern:** You're getting much better pricing now — where does pricing go as we move toward mid-cycle demand?
- **Response:**
  - CEO Runser: core LTL pricing keeps improving on a rational market; deferred renewals +6.3% (best since 3Q22) [book]
  - Spot rates currently exceed contract by 15–20% (ex-fuel); TL contract increases ran low-to-mid single digits in 1Q and are expected low-to-mid double-digit into 2Q/3Q [book]
- **Takeaway:** Pricing is the credible part of the story — disciplined renewals plus a tightening TL backdrop give a real rate runway; the open question is whether volume/weight follow before cost inflation eats it.

**3. Asset-Light profitability durability**  *(J. Bruce Chan, Stifel)*
- **Concern:** Asset-Light productivity looks driven by Managed mix — how do you think about shipment growth and headcount need in brokerage, and what's the spot/contract mix?
- **Response:**
  - CEO Runser: $2.8M (rounded to $3M) non-GAAP OI in Q1 vs $1.5M for all of FY25; record productivity and record-low SG&A/shipment from prior tech investment, growing without adding headcount [book]
  - COO Pinkerton: Asset-Light spot vs contract mix is roughly 50/50, steady through Q1 [book]
- **Takeaway:** The Asset-Light swing looks at least partly structural (Managed mix + productivity), not purely a TL-rate snapback — that's what makes the $40M–$70M 2028 OI target less of a stretch than it looks off a $1.5M FY25 base.

**Read:** The call is a 'the trough is behind us, but early' message with a forward burden of proof. Analysts pressed on whether Q2's above-seasonal OR is fuel vs real strength (management says real), on the pricing runway (credible, +6.3% renewals, spot 15–20% over contract), and on Asset-Light durability (structural Managed mix, not just TL beta). Management's answer is consistent — broad strength + discipline — but the demand inflection itself is admittedly unproven. [book]
