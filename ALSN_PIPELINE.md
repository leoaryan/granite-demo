*Designer and manufacturer of vehicle propulsion solutions — fully automatic transmissions and electrified propulsion for on-highway trucks, buses, motorhomes, defense vehicles and off-highway equipment. Now operates through two business units: legacy Allison Transmission and the Allison Off-Highway Drive & Motion Systems business acquired from Dana on January 1, 2026 (with a centralized Allison Central Group cost center).*

## Task 0 — Executive Summary: The View

The first quarter as a combined company. Reported net sales nearly doubled to $1,406M (+84% y/y) on the addition of $673M from the acquired Allison Off-Highway unit, but the headline GAAP optics are messy: consolidated gross margin fell to 29% from 49% and GAAP EPS dropped to $1.33 from $2.23 (-40%) — almost entirely purchase-price-accounting noise (~$76M of inventory step-up + incremental D&A in COGS, $21-22M intangible amortization, $17M integration costs) [book]. Strip the noise and the operating story is solid: adjusted EBITDA $362M (+22%) at a 26% margin and adjusted diluted EPS $2.57 (+6%) [book]. Legacy Allison Transmission revenue was down 4% to $733M in soft on-highway/medium-duty markets, offset by a +64% Defense quarter [book]. Bull case: synergy capture ($120M run-rate target) + a 27-29% EBITDA-margin path + cash generation. Bear case: end markets remain 'troughy,' the acquired unit ran a segment operating loss of $(21)M, and net leverage is elevated post-deal.

## Task 5 — Earnings Call: Management Discussion & Q&A

### Section 1 — Management Discussion (prepared remarks)

- **Integration & synergies:** Integration 'proceeding in a disciplined and structured manner'; initial synergy realization taking shape with financial benefit beginning later in 2026; reaffirmed $120M annual run-rate synergy target [book]
  > "reaffirmed guidance in achieving our target of $120 million of annual run-rate synergies" — CEO Graziosi
- **End markets:** NA On-Highway 'cautious optimism' with order strength but tariff/EPA uncertainty; Defense extremely strong (+64% y/y); Off-Highway Mining/Construction/Ag demand solid [book]
- **Margins:** Adj EBITDA $362M (+22%) at 26% margin; reiterated 27-29% adjusted-EBITDA-margin target as synergy + end-market recovery lift margins [book]
- **Capital allocation:** Repaid $150M of the revolver; 7th consecutive annual dividend increase ($0.29/qtr); $20M+ buyback; ~$1.2B authorization remaining; targeting 2.0x net leverage [book]

### Section 2 — Q&A (analyst inquiries)

**1. Deal model vs reality + end-market view**  *(Rob Wertheimer, Melius Research)*
- **Concern:** What has changed most positively and negatively vs the deal model since announcement/close, and any change in the troughy end markets?
- **Response:**
  - CEO: the acquisition is 'exceeding expectations' — broader operational footprint more valuable than modeled amid trade/tariff volatility [book]
  - End-market view unchanged — still 'troughy,' but equipment utilization will eventually drive demand; question is timing [book]
- **Takeaway:** Management leans on the footprint/flexibility benefit of the deal in a volatile trade environment while explicitly NOT calling an end-market turn — the recovery is a 'when,' not 'if.'

**2. 27-29% adj EBITDA margin target timing**  *(Tim Thein, Raymond James)*
- **Concern:** When is the 27-29% adjusted-EBITDA-margin target realized, and has the timeline moved post-close?
- **Response:**
  - CEO: 'very comfortable' with the range; near-term issues have no long-term timing impact [book]
  - Still 'very attainable within a few years' — value-capture work has surfaced additional opportunity areas [book]
- **Takeaway:** Margin recovery from the diluted 26% is a multi-year synergy-plus-recovery story, not a 2026 event — the 'few years' framing is the key qualifier the Street must underwrite.

**3. Sequential cadence + synergy capture in 2026**  *(Jerry Revich, Wells Fargo Securities)*
- **Concern:** Do both units step up sequentially Q2 vs Q1 with margins up, and is there a 2Q-vs-1Q cost benefit from synergies?
- **Response:**
  - Transmission and Off-Highway both modeled up sequentially into Q2; Q4 steps down (days / European seasonality); pre-buy is the Q4 swing factor [book]
  - CFO: synergy opportunity size/timing 'has not changed whatsoever'; more detail and run-rate timing to come through the year [book]
- **Takeaway:** Q2 is the proof point for the modeled step-up; synergies are reaffirmed but still un-quantified in-period — management is asking the Street to wait a quarter for the number.

**4. Off-Highway y/y growth + pricing by segment**  *(Tami Zakaria, JPMorgan)*
- **Concern:** How does the $673M Off-Highway revenue compare y/y, and what was price realization by segment?
- **Response:**
  - Off-Highway up 'just over 10%' y/y — mix of EUR FX ($1.07/1.08 → $1.17) and strong Mining/Construction/Ag/India demand [book]
  - Allison Transmission ~325bps of price in-quarter (~that range for the full year); Off-Highway price ~neutral y/y [book]
- **Takeaway:** Off-Highway growth is partly FX-flattered but underlying demand is real; the pricing split (Transmission +325bps vs Off-Highway flat) frames where the margin leverage sits.

**5. Price/cost positioning for the year**  *(Kyle Menges, Citigroup)*
- **Concern:** At ~350bps Transmission price, is price/cost positive for the year, and is Off-Highway price/cost negative if price is flat?
- **Response:**
  - CFO: Transmission y/y price expected to cover inflationary cost; Q1 delta was volume/mix, not price/cost [book]
  - Off-Highway 'pretty neutral' — minor price give-backs offset within the operational/purchasing cost structure [book]
- **Takeaway:** Transmission stays price/cost positive; Off-Highway is managed to neutral via cost-down rather than price — consistent with the synergy/best-cost-country thesis.

**Read:** The call is a 'clean operating quarter, hold the guide' message: management repeatedly reaffirmed the $120M synergy target and 27-29% margin path while declining to quantify in-year synergy or call an end-market bottom [book]. Analysts pressed on exactly the open items — margin-target timing (Thein), sequential cadence + synergy quantification (Revich), and price/cost (Menges) — and got 'few years,' 'unchanged,' and 'more detail later' rather than numbers. The burden of proof shifts to Q2's sequential step-up. [book]
