## Executive Summary — The View

A strong, M&A-led quarter for Goldman: **EPS $17.55 (+24% y/y), net revenue $17.23B (+14%), ROE 19.8%.** The engine was **Global Banking & Markets ($12.74B)**, powered by a **48% surge in investment-banking fees** on a rebound in completed M&A advisory — the cyclical recovery thesis playing out. Equities financing hit records; FICC was the soft spot (−10%, rates/mortgages). Capital return was heavy ($5.0B buybacks + $1.38B dividends; dividend $4.50/sh). The quarter reads as **"the deal cycle is back"** — high-quality, advisory-driven revenue with strong returns and disciplined expenses (efficiency ratio 60.5%, flat).

**Watch:** durability of the IB-fee rebound (backlog ticked *down* slightly); FICC weakness; provision normalization ($315M).
