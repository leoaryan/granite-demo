## Executive Summary — The View

Avery delivered a steady, productivity-driven beat in a soft-demand, deflationary quarter: adjusted EPS $2.47 (+7.4%) on net sales of $2.3B (+7.0% reported, but only +1.1% organic). The growth is **earnings quality, not volume** — Materials productivity + a ~5¢ tariff-pre-buy benefit carried the print while organic demand stayed sluggish (base categories soft, Intelligent Labels down LSD). The story is two-sided: **Materials resilient (EBITDA margin +10bps to 17.8%); Solutions weak (margin −80bps, Intelligent Labels soft)**. Management held the FY26 EPS guide ($9.90–10.20) and leaned into buybacks. The Wiliot +$75M investment deepens the RFID/Intelligent-Labels moat — a long-game bet, not a this-quarter driver.

**Watch:** the ~5¢ pre-buy benefit unwinds in 2Q (a known headwind management flagged); Intelligent Labels adoption pace; deflation-driven price reductions vs. raw-material inflation.

## Task 5: Earnings Call — Management Discussion & Q&A

**Management discussion (prepared remarks):**
- Beat driven by **MSD volume/mix growth + adjusted EPS +7% y/y** — productivity playbook in a dynamic environment.
- **Wiliot: incremental +$75M investment** — deepens the partnership, dedicated go-to-market, strengthens the enterprise Intelligent Labels platform. A strategic moat investment.
- Organic sales ~+2% (Materials), MSD volume/mix offset by deflation-related price reductions.
- Logistics: lapping outsized 2025 volume/share; proactively managing the comp.
- Intelligent Labels down LSD vs a high pre-tariff PY comp — but "encouraging"; FY26 IL growth expected to outpace 2025.
- Pricing to offset raw-material inflation; relentless productivity/cost focus.

**Q&A (analysts: Baird, BofA, JPMorgan, BMO, UBS, Jefferies, Truist, Raymond James, Citi, Deutsche Bank):**
- **Pre-buy quantification / 2Q unwind:** ~5¢ March pre-buy benefit; expected to "largely unwind" in 2Q — the central modeling question.
- **Intelligent Labels trajectory:** soft this Q (logistics demand, destocking); management holds the full-year outpace-2025 view; Wiliot as the long-game.
- **Deflation vs pricing:** mid-single-digit volume/mix offset by deflation-related price reductions — the Materials margin mechanic.
- **Solutions margin (−80bps EBITDA):** higher employee costs + lower base-category volumes + growth investments more than offset productivity/price-cost gains.
- **Capital allocation:** disciplined buybacks ($61M) given below-target leverage; balance sheet at 2.4x.
