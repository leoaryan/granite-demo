# EquipmentShare.com, Inc. (EQPT) — Q1 2026 (reported May 13, 2026; call May 14, 2026)

> All financials are sourced from the FactSet book (tagged `[book]`); exec bio is web (`[web]`). DRAFT / not yet analyst-blessed. Tasks 1–7 line up with the Gemini 7-task run; **Task 8 (Drivers loop) is our addition — Gemini does not produce it.**

*Digitally-native equipment rental platform serving the largest jobsites nationwide. Two core segments: Equipment Rental & Services Operations (full-service branch rental — the ~77% growth engine) and Equipment Sales (new/used equipment, much of it placed into the capital-light OWN Program where third parties own fleet EquipmentShare deploys and manages). Built around T3, a proprietary operating system that ties telematics, access control, service and utilization data into one platform — the differentiation pitch on megaprojects (data centers, manufacturing, infrastructure). Founded 2015, Columbia MO; IPO'd Jan 23, 2026.*

## Task 0 — Executive Summary: The View

A clean beat-and-raise from a hyper-growth organic compounder. Total revenue $989M (+38%) with Rental Segment revenue $764M (+37%); Adjusted Core EBITDA $399M (+38%), beating the ~$325M Street bar by ~$74M, and consolidated gross margin actually EXPANDED ~109bps to 29.0% [book]. Management raised the full-year guide across the board — FY26 revenue to $5,147–5,575M and Adjusted Core EBITDA to $1,883–1,995M (+$70M at the midpoint) — on maturing branches and 'unprecedented' megaproject demand [book]. The catch: still a GAAP net loss of $(29)M and a $200M operating-cash burn (vs $51M PY), so the bull case is unit-economics-and-maturation (mature sites at 55% TTM EBITDA margin) while the bear case is the cash burn, the OWN-model complexity, and a July lock-up overhang — which is why the stock fell ~6% on a clean print.

## Task 5 — Earnings Call: Management Discussion & Q&A

### Section 1 — Management Discussion (prepared remarks)

- **Quarter framing:** Rental Segment revenue grew 37% to $764M on 'strong customer demand across industrial, infrastructure, data center, and advanced manufacturing'; full-year outlook raised across the board [book]
  > "We delivered a strong first quarter and are raising our 2026 outlook across the board." — CEO Jabbok Schlacks
- **Unit economics:** Trailing-twelve-month mature rental location adjusted-EBITDA margin was 55%, framed as the 'embedded earnings power' of the footprint as it matures [book]
- **T3 / differentiation:** T3 positioned as the 'live operating layer across equipment, access control, service, utilization, and jobsite activity' — the moat on large complex jobsites [book]
  > "large, complex jobsites need more than equipment availability. They need visibility, control, and faster execution." — President Willy Schlacks
- **Footprint:** 407 operational locations with 22 opened in Q1; OEC under management up to $9,065M (owned + OWN Program + op-leases) [book]

### Section 2 — Q&A (analyst inquiries)

**1. Adjusted Core EBITDA / SBC add-back methodology**  *(Kenneth Newman, KeyBanc Capital Markets)*
- **Concern:** The adj Core EBITDA guide adds back ~$19M of SBC this quarter — historically included; is the $70M EBITDA guide raise really just driven by the higher SBC add-back?
- **Response:**
  - Of the $19M of SBC, $17M is the IPO Founders Awards (PSU tied to up to a $90B market cap), excluded because proxying to $90B isn't reflective of current earnings power [book]
  - The SBC exclusion is consistent y/y; the $70M raise had no impact from the SBC change — driven by Rental/locations/equip-sales [book]
- **Takeaway:** Management defused the 'low-quality raise' worry — the $70M is operational, not an accounting add-back — but the Founders Award PSU is a real ongoing dilution/expense item investors must model from the 10-Q footnote.

**2. Rental gross margin / same-store performance**  *(Jerry Revich (for Gary Liebowitz), Wells Fargo Securities)*
- **Concern:** Rental gross margin was up ~200bps y/y — what was the same-store/like-for-like for mature sites and the tailwind from new-start drag declining as the footprint grows?
- **Response:**
  - Rental segment revenue vs direct COGS showed a 'nice lift' in gross margin plus SG&A leverage, both on top of volume [book]
  - Mature sites (>24mo) ran a 55% TTM contribution margin; new sites are 'ramping nicely' and the mix drove Q1 [book]
- **Takeaway:** The margin expansion is maturation + leverage, not pricing — consistent with the 'stable pricing backdrop' comment; the bull's proof that the model scales profitably.

**3. Construction macro**  *(Aaron Kimson, Citizens JMP Securities)*
- **Concern:** Any noticeable change in the commercial-construction macro since mid-March, and what are the drivers?
- **Response:**
  - Management described a 'K-type recovery' — unprecedented demand from onshoring, manufacturing, data-center megaprojects, while residential/commercial (~11% of customers) is stable-to-softening in some geographies [book]
- **Takeaway:** EQPT is deliberately levered to the strong leg of a bifurcated market (~87% non-resi/industrial per Citizens JMP); the growth is concentrated in the megaproject pipeline, which is also the concentration risk.

**4. Guidance composition**  *(Scott Schneeberger, Oppenheimer)*
- **Concern:** Revenue guide raised $100M but Rental only ~$55M — what's behind the remaining ~$45M, and is 'other' revenue a sizable contributor?
- **Response:**
  - The increase was driven primarily by Equipment Rental and Equipment Sales revenue; EQPT now breaks out the Equipment Sales segment EBITDA contribution separately in the guide [book]
  - Net leverage 2.8x (from 3.2x); targeting low-3s at year-end, mid-to-low-2s long term [book]
- **Takeaway:** The raise leans on Rental but ~$18M of incremental EBITDA is lower-margin equipment sales (Baird) — a slightly softer mix than the headline, which is part of why the stock didn't reward the beat.

**Read:** The call was a 'demand is unprecedented, the model is maturing' message met with skeptical, mechanics-focused questions — analysts pressed on the SBC add-back, the composition of the guide raise, same-store margin, and the macro, rather than the headline beat [book]. Management's answers were specific (the $70M raise is operational; mature sites at 55%; macro is K-shaped/non-resi), which is why the desks stayed bullish even as the stock fell — the sell-off was about positioning and the July lock-up, not the substance of the print.
